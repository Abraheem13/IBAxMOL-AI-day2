PROCUREMENT PROCESS — Goods Purchase Request (PPRA Open Competition)
=====================================================================
Scope: routine technical goods, contract value within PPRA Rule 12-21 threshold
Anonymised for training. Use placeholders [DEPT], [VENDOR-X], [ITEM-Y].

STEP 1 — Requisition raised
- Requesting Department prepares Purchase Requisition (PR) in SAP with: item code, 
  quantity, estimated unit price, business justification, budget code.
- Time: 20-40 minutes.
- Friction: vendors and item codes not always up-to-date in SAP master data.

STEP 2 — Department Head approval
- PR routed to Department Head for endorsement.
- SLA target: 48 hours.
- Actual: 2-7 days. Frequent reminders needed.

STEP 3 — Procurement Officer triage
- Procurement Officer screens PR: technical-spec completeness, budget code validity, 
  PPRA compliance threshold.
- Time: 15-30 minutes per PR.
- Returns to Department for revision if incomplete (rework loop frequency: ~30%).

STEP 4 — Method-of-procurement decision
- Procurement Officer determines: open competition / limited tendering / direct 
  contracting (PPRA Rule 36).
- Documentation: PPRA method justification memo prepared.

STEP 5 — Tender preparation
- Officer drafts the tender document: scope, technical specs, evaluation criteria, 
  contract terms.
- Time: 4-8 hours per tender.

STEP 6 — Tender advertisement
- Tender uploaded to PPRA portal + published in two daily newspapers (English + Urdu).
- Submission window: minimum 15 days (per PPRA Rule 13).

STEP 7 — Bid submission window
- Bidders submit technical and financial bids (separate sealed envelopes).
- Pre-bid clarification meeting often held mid-window.

STEP 8 — Comparative statement preparation
- Procurement Officer prepares technical evaluation table and financial comparison.
- Time: 90 minutes to 4 hours depending on number of bidders.
- Manually transcribed from bid documents to Excel.

STEP 9 — Technical evaluation
- Tender Committee meets to score technical bids against criteria.
- Time: half-day meeting.
- Pass-fail threshold typically 70/100.

STEP 10 — Financial evaluation + award recommendation
- Financial bids of technically-qualified bidders opened publicly.
- Award recommendation drafted: lowest-evaluated responsive bidder.

STEP 11 — Purchase Order generation
- PO created in SAP with: vendor, item, quantity, unit price, total, delivery terms, 
  payment terms, tax info.
- PO emailed to vendor; read-receipt requested.
- Friction: vendor acknowledgement chase if no read-receipt.

STEP 12 — Vendor delivery commitment
- Vendor confirms delivery date.
- Sometimes requires advance payment (LC, advance against PO).

STEP 13 — Goods receipt
- Stores receives shipment; physical inspection vs PO.
- Goods Receipt Note (GRN) created in SAP.

STEP 14 — GRN cross-check
- Procurement Officer or Stores verifies GRN vs PO: quantity, condition, dates.
- Time: 30-60 minutes per GRN.
- Discrepancies trigger vendor correspondence.

STEP 15 — Invoice receipt from vendor
- Vendor submits invoice with: PO ref, GRN ref, tax certificate.
- Format varies: PDF, paper, email body.

STEP 16 — Three-way match (PO + GRN + Invoice)
- Procurement / Finance jointly verify amounts, line items, tax handling.
- Time: 60 minutes per invoice.
- Variances paused for resolution.

STEP 17 — Payment approval
- Budget Holder + Finance Manager sign off; release to Treasury for payment.

STEP 18 — Payment release
- Treasury releases payment per terms (typically 30-90 days from invoice).
- Vendor remittance advice sent.
- GL posting completed.

=====================================================================
PROCESS METRICS (typical for medium operator)
- Total cycle time: 45-90 days from PR to payment
- Frequency: 80-150 routine POs per month
- Human-hour cost per PO: 12-18 hours across all roles
- Rework rate: ~25% of PRs returned at Step 3
- Manager-approval-delay percentage of total cycle time: ~30%
