HSE INCIDENT NOTIFICATION WORKFLOW
========================================
Scope: from incident detection to OGRA Section 26 disclosure
Anonymised for training. Placeholders: [SITE], [ASSET-X], [TECH-A].

STEP 1 — Incident occurs
- Could be: gas release, leak, fire, slip-trip-fall, near-miss, environmental release.
- Detection methods: gas detector alarm, visual observation, operator report, 
  contractor report.

STEP 2 — Immediate response
- Shift Supervisor or Control Room notified within minutes.
- Personnel evacuation if needed; isolation actions; emergency response team mobilised.

STEP 3 — Initial assessment by Shift Supervisor
- Severity classification (preliminary): Category 1 / 2 / 3 / Near-Miss.
- Verbal escalation to HSE Officer on duty.

STEP 4 — HSE Officer on-site
- Arrives within 30 minutes (target).
- Photographs scene, interviews witnesses, takes initial readings.
- Initiates internal incident form 02-A (currently paper or PDF).

STEP 5 — Internal incident form lodged
- Form fields: date/time, location, persons involved, sequence of events, 
  immediate actions, witnesses, attachments.
- Lodged in HSE incident register (often Excel-based with macro tracking).

STEP 6 — Severity confirmation
- HSE Manager reviews initial form; confirms or adjusts severity classification.
- Determines whether OGRA Section 26 disclosure is triggered.

STEP 7 — Verbal briefing to GM Operations
- Same-day verbal briefing.
- Often via phone or in-person; not always recorded.

STEP 8 — Written briefing to GM Operations + Director (HSE)
- Within 24 hours.
- Word document, distributed by email.

STEP 9 — Investigation team formed (if Category 2 or higher)
- Lead investigator named.
- Witness interview schedule prepared.
- Evidence-preservation protocol initiated.

STEP 10 — Root-cause investigation
- 5-Whys, fishbone (Ishikawa), barrier analysis.
- Time: 3-10 days depending on severity.

STEP 11 — OGRA Section 26 disclosure preparation
- Drafted by Compliance Officer based on investigation findings.
- Reviewed by HSE Manager, GM Operations, Legal.
- Approved by Director or designated authority.
- Submitted within 48 hours (per regulation).

STEP 12 — CAPA plan
- Corrective actions and preventive actions identified.
- Each action: owner, target date, verification method.
- Logged in CAPA tracker (often Excel or in CMMS).

STEP 13 — CAPA execution
- Owners execute their actions.
- Status reviewed weekly until closure.

STEP 14 — CAPA verification
- HSE auditor verifies each closed action.
- Sign-off recorded in tracker.

STEP 15 — Lessons-learned brief
- Compiled by HSE Manager.
- Circulated to all sites within 14 days.
- Toolbox-talk material prepared.

STEP 16 — Incident closure
- All CAPA actions verified, lessons circulated, OGRA filing acknowledged.
- Incident marked closed in register.

========================================
PROCESS METRICS
- Frequency: 30-60 minor incidents/month; 2-5 reportable/month for medium operator
- Average cycle (Cat 2): 30-45 days from incident to closure
- Step 11 (OGRA disclosure prep) often runs to 36-44 hours within the 48-hour window
- Reminders are heavy: most slippage is in Step 13 (CAPA execution by named owners)
