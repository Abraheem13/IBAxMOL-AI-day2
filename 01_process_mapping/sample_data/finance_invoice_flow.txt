VENDOR INVOICE TO PAYMENT WORKFLOW
========================================
Scope: routine vendor invoice processing for goods/services
Anonymised. Placeholders: [VENDOR-X], [INVOICE-N], [AMOUNT].

STEP 1 — Vendor issues invoice
- Vendor prepares invoice referencing PO + GRN + tax certificate.

STEP 2 — Invoice receipt at AP department
- Receipt channels (multiple, inconsistent): email PDF, paper hand-delivery, 
  email body text, vendor portal upload, occasionally WhatsApp photo.
- AP Clerk catalogues in incoming-invoice register.

STEP 3 — Three-way match (PO + GRN + Invoice)
- AP Clerk opens SAP, retrieves PO and GRN, compares against invoice line by line:
  vendor, item description, quantity, unit price, total, tax, currency.
- Variances flagged.
- Time per invoice: 5-15 minutes for clean; 30-60 minutes for variance.

STEP 4 — Withholding tax determination
- Vendor's WHT category looked up in master data (sometimes outdated).
- Applicable tax rate applied per FBR schedule.
- WHT certificate prepared for vendor on payment.

STEP 5 — Invoice posted to SAP
- AP Clerk creates payable entry.

STEP 6 — Budget-holder approval
- Routed to Budget Holder / Project Manager via SAP workflow OR via email.
- SLA target: 48 hours. Actual: often 5-7 days. Frequent reminders.

STEP 7 — AP Supervisor verification
- Verifies match, tax, posting accuracy.

STEP 8 — Finance Manager approval
- Approves for payment scheduling.

STEP 9 — Payment scheduling
- Treasury determines payment date per terms (typically 30-90 days from invoice).
- Cash position reviewed.

STEP 10 — Payment file generation
- Bank-specific payment file generated (often Excel/CSV exported from SAP, then 
  formatted to bank template).
- Frequency: typically weekly batch.

STEP 11 — Bank file submission
- Treasury uploads to bank portal (HBL, MCB, Bank Alfalah, etc.) or sends to bank 
  relationship manager.
- Manual verification + sign-off by authorised signatory.

STEP 12 — Payment execution
- Bank processes; payment hits vendor account.
- Bank confirmation received.

STEP 13 — Vendor remittance advice
- AP sends remittance advice to vendor with payment ref, amount, invoices paid.
- Format: emailed PDF or Word document.

STEP 14 — Tax certificate to vendor
- WHT certificate generated and sent (separately or with remittance advice).

STEP 15 — GL posting
- Payment entry reconciled in GL.
- AP balance cleared.

STEP 16 — Audit log update
- Audit trail captured: who approved, when, supporting documents archived.

========================================
PROCESS METRICS (typical for medium operator)
- Frequency: 200-600 vendor invoices/month
- Human-hour cost per invoice: 25-45 minutes across roles
- Cycle time (clean invoice): 35-50 days
- Cycle time (variance/exception): 60-90 days
- Manager approval delay (Step 6) often 30-40% of total cycle time
- Bank file errors: ~5% of batches require correction
