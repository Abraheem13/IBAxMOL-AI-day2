MAINTENANCE WORK-ORDER WORKFLOW
========================================
Scope: routine corrective maintenance (low-medium priority)
Anonymised. Placeholders: [ASSET-X], [TECH-A], [VENDOR-Y].

STEP 1 — Defect identification
- Source: condition monitoring, operator report, inspection finding, predictive 
  system alert.
- Logged in CMMS as a notification.

STEP 2 — Work-order creation
- CMMS converts notification to a work order (WO).
- WO fields: asset ID, problem description, priority, target completion.

STEP 3 — Planner triage and categorisation
- Maintenance Planner reviews; sets priority, work type (mechanical / electrical / 
  instrumentation), required skills.
- Assigns to Supervisor.
- Friction: WO often sits in queue 1-3 days waiting for planner attention.

STEP 4 — Resource and parts planning
- Planner identifies: required parts, special tools, manpower hours, contractor needs.
- Parts availability checked in SAP — frequently inaccurate vs actual stock.

STEP 5 — Parts requisition
- If parts not in stock: reservation or purchase requisition raised.
- If urgent: emergency procurement (separate workflow, longer).

STEP 6 — Permit-to-Work (PTW)
- For hot work, confined space, electrical isolation, height work: PTW required.
- Currently: paper form signed by HSE, Operations, Maintenance.
- Multi-signature loop; often 1-3 days.

STEP 7 — Job-safety analysis
- JSA / risk assessment completed for the job.
- Toolbox talk delivered to assigned crew.

STEP 8 — Crew assignment
- Supervisor assigns specific technicians and contractors.

STEP 9 — Parts and tools collected from stores
- Storeman issues; technician collects; signature in store register or scanner log.

STEP 10 — Isolation and lockout-tagout
- Operations isolates the equipment.
- Technician applies lockout-tagout devices.

STEP 11 — Execution
- Job carried out per JSA.
- Photos taken before/during/after.

STEP 12 — Quality check
- Maintenance Supervisor inspects completed work.
- Functional test performed.

STEP 13 — Return-to-service
- Lockout-tagout removed.
- Operations brings equipment back online.
- Performance monitored for first 24-72 hours.

STEP 14 — CMMS closure
- Technician updates WO in CMMS: actual hours, parts used, observations, photos.
- Friction: technicians often delay this entry; field data often missing or incomplete.

STEP 15 — Reliability review
- Reliability Engineer reviews completed WOs weekly.
- Patterns flagged for root-cause review or preventive plan update.

========================================
PROCESS METRICS
- Frequency: 80-200 routine WOs/month for medium operator
- Avg cycle time (low priority): 5-12 days
- Avg cycle time (high priority): 1-3 days
- Permit-loop time often accounts for 30-50% of total cycle time
- CMMS closure delays distort MTTR (mean time to repair) metric by 20-40%
