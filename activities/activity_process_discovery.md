# 🔍 Activity — Process Discovery in Your Own Function

**Time required:** 30 minutes
**Format:** Small groups of 3 from the same function
**Purpose:** Find ONE real automation candidate in your own work, mapped well enough to build it tomorrow

---

## 🎯 The mission

By the end of this activity, every participant has identified **one specific process** in their own function that:

- Currently takes 30+ minutes per occurrence
- Happens at least weekly
- Has at least one clear automation opportunity
- Can be sketched as a swim-lane on a single page

This is your **shovel-ready** automation candidate. Day 2 should end with you having one.

---

## 📋 Step 1 — Brainstorm (10 minutes)

In your group, each person silently lists 3-5 processes they personally touch each week. Don't filter; list everything.

Example brainstorm output:
- Generating monthly KPI report from SAP exports
- Routing budget-overrun requests for approval
- Following up on overdue CAPA actions
- Reconciling spare-parts deliveries with POs
- Preparing weekly safety stats for HSE meeting
- Filing tender clarification queries during open tender windows
- Onboarding new vendors (creating master data, KYC, banking details)

---

## 📋 Step 2 — Score each candidate (10 minutes)

Score each process on these dimensions (1-5 scale):

| Process | Frequency | Time per run | Reversibility | Total |
|---------|-----------|--------------|---------------|-------|
| | (per month) | (minutes) | (low=irreversible/high=easy undo) | |

The best automation candidates score **HIGH on frequency**, **HIGH on time per run**, **HIGH on reversibility** (because if the automation misbehaves, you can fix it without consequence).

The candidate with the highest total is your **shovel-ready** project.

---

## 📋 Step 3 — Map it (10 minutes)

On paper or a flipchart, draw the swim-lane for **just your highest-scoring candidate**.

Rows (roles + systems): 3-7 max.
Columns (steps): 5-12 max.

Mark in red:
- Every handoff that waits > 24 hours
- Every step that's a copy-paste from one system to another
- Every step that's a reminder/follow-up email

Each red mark is an automation opportunity.

---

## 📋 Step 4 — Present (one person per group, 60 seconds)

Each group shares:
1. The process they chose
2. The current cycle time
3. The single highest-impact automation step they identified
4. Their best guess at time saved if automated (hours per month)

The room votes on which group has the most compelling case. Winning group leads the capstone.

---

## 🎓 What to take away

Three rules:

1. **The most valuable automation is usually in the friction you've stopped noticing.** Senior staff filter out routine pain; map it.
2. **Reversibility beats sophistication.** A simple flow that sends harmless reminders saves 20 hours/month and never causes harm. Build that first.
3. **Your swim-lane is the spec.** Hand it to IT or the Power Automate hands-on session — they will build from your map directly.
